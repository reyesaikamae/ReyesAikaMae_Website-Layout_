Nature and importance of the study

Chinese cabbagecabbage (Brassica pekinensi) was one of the important and most common leafy vegetable sold in the market. It can be growth either for commercial or small scale farming. It resembles the head lettuce in many ways. The leave is soft, thin, light green, broad to oblong and usually wrinkled. It forms a head that was less compact than the cabbage. The leaves may be short, stout, tall and cylindrical in shape depending on the varities. Asian farmers prefer to raise chinese cabbage because of its rapid growth

Chinese cabbage has higher vitamin C content than citrus and it is rich with minerals. The crop also contain 2.03% of protein, 0.28% fat, 4.13% carbohydrates and 271 calories per kilo of food (Elayda, 1935). Generally, Chinese cabbage grows well in medium to heavy soils. However, higher yield was obtained when planted in friable and fertile soil. Application of feltilizer ether organic or inorganic in forms generally improved growth of plants thus, promote higher yield.
